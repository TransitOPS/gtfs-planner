Our Work

You have two versions of the same station data.

Ours is the version your team enriched — new entrances, pathways, wayfinding nodes. Incoming is the updated upstream feed — corrected coordinates, renamed platforms, revised levels.

This tool compares them station by station, not row by row. A new entrance, its level, and its connecting walkway are shown as one operation — because that is what they are.

How a review works

1
Pick a station from the left.
Stations are sorted by urgency. Collisions — where both sides changed the same thing — appear first because they need your judgment.
2
Review grouped operations, not individual edits.
Each card represents a logical change: "2 new entrances with walkway connections" — not 8 separate row modifications across 3 CSV files. You see the infrastructure change, not the data plumbing.
3
Decide: apply incoming, or keep ours.
Each operation is classified — incoming correction, our addition, collision, or new upstream — so you know what kind of judgment is needed before you read the details.

Four types of change

Incoming Correction

Upstream fixed something: coordinates, names, accessibility data. Usually apply these — they improve the foundation your enrichment depends on.

Our Addition

Entrances, pathways, and nodes your team added. These are safe to keep — scan for completeness, but action is rarely needed.

Collision

Both sides changed the same thing independently. Rare, but requires careful review — neither version can be assumed correct.

Incoming Addition

Upstream added something new that your version doesn't have. Worth reviewing, typically uncommon.
